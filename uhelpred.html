Conservation status

India has protected 20 areas with known or possible red panda populations in Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and West Bengal such as Khangchendzonga, Namdapha and Singalila National Parks, and a coordinated conservation policy for the red panda has been developed.

How you can help

• Stop Deforestation

• Mindfully buy wooden items to decrease wood usage.

• Decrease the amount of pollution produced to help the forests.

• Do not encourage or promote products made with red panda parts

• Spread Awareness

• Spread awareness by sharing this website with others or just spreading the word around.

• Promote NGO's that focus on saving red pandas like,
                  Red Panda Network
                  KATs Protection Of Red Panda’s

• Adopt A Red Panda
• Adopt a Red Panda if available in your local zoo or adopt at
                  WWF
                  Project Panda
