Conservation status

Because of the Red Panda's secretive nature and nocturnal habits, observation of it is very difficult. India has protected 20 areas with known or possible red panda populations in Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and West Bengal such as Khangchendzonga, Namdapha and Singalila National Parks, and a coordinated conservation policy for the red panda and as new eco-friendly alternatives for deforestation have been developed, Red panda population trends are becoming less steep.


How you can help

Stop Deforestation
You can buy less wooden and boycott wooden tools and items. You can also decrease the amount of pollution you produce to help the forests.
Spread Awareness
You can spread awareness by sharing this website with others or just by spreading the word. You can also promote NGO's that focus on saving trees like, Project Green Hands and Team Trees.
Adopt A Red Panda
You can adopt a Red Panda if available in your local zoo or you can adopt at WWF or Pandas International.
